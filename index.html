Symposia Overview

The upcoming Tafakari Symposium, "Theological Education with Informal Settlement Leaders in Nairobi: Pedagogy from Below", will take place on 27th February 2025 in Nairobi, Kenya, in partnership with the Center for Urban Mission.

This symposium will explore the transformative role of theological education in empowering informal settlement leaders through a bottom-up, community-centered approach. Participants will engage in discussions on:

  • Theology from below: Understanding faith and ministry in marginalized communities.
  • Contextual theological education: Adapting pedagogy to serve informal settlements effectively.
  • Urban mission and social transformation: The church’s role in justice, advocacy, and community renewal.

This interactivee event will bring together theologians, church leaders, and practitioners committed to reshaping theological education to better serve urban informal settlements.

Pre-Event Reflection: Preparing for Transformative Dialogue

As we approach the Tafakari Symposium 2025, we are invited to reflect on the significance of theological education in informal settlements and how pedagogy from below can reshape our understanding of ministry, learning, and community transformation.

For decades, theological education has often followed a top-down model, where formal institutions dictate the frameworks of learning. However, in the realities of Nairobi’s informal settlements, faith is not merely theoretical—it is deeply practical, lived out in the struggles, resilience, and daily experiences of marginalized communities.

This symposium seeks to challenge and transform conventional theological approaches by asking:

  • How can we create theological education that listens to and learns from grassroots leaders?
  • What does “pedagogy from below” look like in practice, and how can it empower communities?
  • How can urban theology respond to issues of justice, poverty, and social transformation?

As participants, we are encouraged to come with open hearts and minds—not just to share knowledge, but to listen deeply, engage authentically, and co-create new models of learning that reflect the realities and aspirations of informal settlement leaders.

Next Steps Before the Event:

  • Reflect on your own experiences of theological learning—has it been accessible and relevant to grassroots realities?
  • Engage with writings or case studies on theology and informal settlements.
  • Come ready to exchange ideas, share stories, and embrace new ways of thinking about faith and education.
